A.R.Rahman

About

A.R. Rahman is a world-renowned musician, composer, and producer from India. Born on January 6, 1967, in Chennai (formerly known as Madras), he started his music career at the age of 11 as a keyboard player in the orchestra of famous Indian composer Ilaiyaraaja. He shot to international fame in the 1990s with his work in the Tamil film "Roja", for which he won his first National Film Award. He has since won numerous awards and accolades, including two Academy Awards, two Grammy Awards, a BAFTA Award, and a Golden Globe. Apart from his work in films, Rahman has also composed music for numerous albums and collaborated with several artists from around the world. He has been recognized for his humanitarian efforts as well and was appointed as a UN Goodwill Ambassador in 2017. Rahman is known for his unique fusion of Indian classical music with electronic music and world music elements, creating a distinctive and innovative sound. His music has been described as a bridge between East and West, and has inspired a generation of musicians and composers.

Awards

  • Academy Award (Oscar) for Best Original Score - Slumdog Millionaire (2009)
  • Academy Award (Oscar) for Best Original Song - "Jai Ho" from Slumdog Millionaire (2009)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score - Slumdog Millionaire (2009)
  • Grammy Award for Best Compilation Soundtrack for Visual Media - Slumdog Millionaire (2010)
  • Grammy Award for Best Song Written for Visual Media - "Jai Ho" from Slumdog Millionaire (2010)
  • BAFTA Award for Best Original Score - Slumdog Millionaire (2009)
  • National Film Award for Best Music Direction - Roja (1993), Minsara Kanavu (1997), Lagaan (2002), Kannathil Muthamittal (2003), Guru (2007), and Kaaviya Thalaivan (2014)
  • Filmfare Award for Best Music Director - Rangeela (1995), Taal (1999), Lagaan (2002), Rang De Basanti (2006), Rockstar (2012), and 2.0 (2019)
These are just a few of the many awards that A.R. Rahman has won throughout his illustrious career in the music industry.
